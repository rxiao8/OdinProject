Description:

A classic appetizer to have is scallion pancakes. The crispy outer texture mixed with a chewy dough is a must have experience, and pairing that with the flavorful dipping sauce really completes the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of flour
  • 1/2 cup of warm water
  • 1/2 of hot water
  • sesame oil
  • 1 cup of diced scallions
  • 1 tsp of salt and pepper
  • Olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of diced ginger
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp of rice vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Combine the dry ingredients exculding the scallions and ginger.
  2. Add the hot water to flour and quickly mix. Once all the water has been soaked, pour in the warm water.
  3. Mix until it forms and dough, then transfer to a flat surface and knead for 10 minutes.
  4. Pour a little bit of olive oil into a bowl and coat the inside. Place the dough into the bowl and roll it so all sides made contact with the oil. Cover the bowl and let it sit for an hour.
  5. Transfer the dough to a flat surface and split into quaters.
  6. Flatten each quater until 1/2 inch thick and sprinkle some scallions and sesame oil on top. Leave a few amount of scallions for the dipping sauce.
  7. Roll each one into a long strand before taking one end and wrapping the dough around itself like a lollipop.
  8. Flatten the circular dough until it is about 1/3 inch thick.
  9. Heat a pan to medium heat before pouring in olive oil. Place one of the dough onto the pan and cook 5 minutes on each side. Do the same for the rest.
  10. For the dipping sauce, combine the soy sauce, rice vinegar, and ginger in a sauce bowl. Top off with remaining scallions.
